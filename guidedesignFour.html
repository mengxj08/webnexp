STAGE 4 : Adjust duration of task blocks

The purpose of this stage is to adjust the amount of time your entire experiment takes. This may require you to adjust the number of independent variables you test, or the arrangement of the task blocks or you may even have to reconsider how to test your solution. Experimental design incorporates many elements and it is normal to try different formats before you find the design that is effective and can be conducted in a reasonable amount of time.

Terms used in this section:

Trial: each performance of the task that will be measured. A minimum of three trials per condition is recommended.

Block: a group of trials.

Condition: the unique set of independent variables that will be used in each block of trials. Changing the number of independent variables will change the number of participants required to test all the possible combinations.

Instructions:

Step 1: Estimate the time for each trial

Step 2: Review the resulting time for each block

Step 3: Review the trials and blocks so that the main part of the experiment is within the recommended 45 minutes.

Step 4: If the experiment is too long, you may have to return to Stage 2 and Stage 3 to adjust the number of independent variables, test conditions and the order of tasks. Feel free to adjust the various elements of the experiment until the experiment falls within a reasonable time span.
